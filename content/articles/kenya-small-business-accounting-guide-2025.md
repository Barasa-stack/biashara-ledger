Running a small business in Kenya comes with unique accounting challenges. From KRA compliance to M-Pesa reconciliation, here is your complete guide to staying on top of your finances in 2025.

## Why Proper Accounting Matters

Many small business owners in Kenya treat accounting as an afterthought. But proper bookkeeping is the foundation of business growth. It helps you track cash flow, prepare for tax season, and make informed decisions.

## Key Areas to Focus On

### 1. KRA Compliance
Every business registered in Kenya must file taxes with KRA. This includes:
- **Income Tax** — File annual returns by June 30th
- **VAT** — File monthly if your turnover exceeds KES 5 million
- **PAYE** — File monthly if you have employees

### 2. M-Pesa Reconciliation
With the majority of Kenyan businesses accepting M-Pesa payments, reconciling your M-Pesa statement with your sales records is critical. Manual reconciliation is time-consuming and error-prone.

### 3. Inventory Management
For retail and wholesale businesses, tracking stock levels, reorder points, and supplier orders is essential to avoid stockouts and overstocking.
